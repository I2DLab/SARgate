SAR Notes

The SAR Notes section provides an interactive annotation workspace for subset scaffolds. It is designed to support medicinal-chemistry interpretation by allowing the user to attach persistent notes both to the full subset and to individual R-group anchor positions.

Subset-Oriented Annotation

The left panel lists the available subsets. Selecting one subset loads its representative scaffold into the central workspace together with any notes already associated with that subset. A General notes field is available for comments that apply to the entire subset, such as synthetic considerations, SAR hypotheses, or prioritisation remarks.

R-Group Notes

The scaffold image contains interactive triggers positioned over the R-group anchors. Clicking one of these anchors opens a small movable note window associated with that specific position. These notes are useful for recording local SAR observations, for example preferred substituents, steric constraints, tolerated polarity changes, or unresolved design questions for a given R position.

Notes are stored separately for each subset, so annotations remain aligned with the correct scaffold and do not overwrite notes belonging to other subsets that may share a related core.

Persistence

SAR Notes are automatically saved to a JSON file in the current report directory. This means that notes are preserved across sessions and can be reopened together with the corresponding job results without any manual export step.
